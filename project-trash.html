Overview

Building an arcade game around a green theme

Project Trash is an arcade game developed at Futuregames with a team of 14. The project centred on creating a fun, engaging arcade experience with an environmental — or "green" — theme woven into the core concept.

As the UX Designer and UX Researcher on the team, the focus was on ensuring the game felt intuitive, the green theme was communicated clearly through gameplay, and that the overall player experience remained engaging and cohesive across a larger team collaboration.

Problem

How do you make a themed arcade game feel cohesive with 14 people?

Working with a large team of 14 introduced coordination challenges. Every contributor needed to share the same vision for the green theme and understand how their work connected to the player experience. The arcade genre demands instant readability and satisfying feedback loops — layering a thematic message on top without compromising gameplay required careful UX thinking.

  • Coordinating a shared vision across a 14-person team
  • Integrating a green/environmental theme without sacrificing arcade fun
  • Ensuring instant readability and intuitive controls for the arcade genre
  • Maintaining design consistency across many contributors

Process

Research, prototyping, and playtesting at scale

The process began with research into how environmental themes have been successfully incorporated into games without feeling heavy-handed. Competitive analysis of arcade games helped establish best practices for controls, pacing, and feedback.

As UX Researcher, playtesting sessions were conducted throughout development to validate design decisions. Working with such a large team meant establishing clear UX guidelines early so that everyone — artists, programmers, and designers — was aligned on the player experience goals.

Research

Studied arcade game conventions and how environmental themes can be embedded into gameplay mechanics and visual design without disrupting the core fun loop.

Playtesting

Ran iterative playtesting sessions to identify friction points, validate the green theme's clarity, and ensure the controls met arcade-level responsiveness.

Process Documentation / Playtest Insights

Solution

A themed arcade experience that stays true to its genre

The final game successfully blends the green theme into its arcade mechanics. The environmental message is embedded in the gameplay itself rather than being a layer on top, making it feel natural and integrated. Visual design reinforces the theme through colour palette, environment art, and interactive elements.

UX guidelines established early in the project helped maintain consistency across all contributions. Clear visual hierarchy, responsive controls, and satisfying feedback loops kept the arcade feel front and centre while the green theme gave the experience a unique identity.

Final Game Screenshots / UI Design

Outcome

Delivering a cohesive game with a large team

Project Trash was completed and published on itch.io, with additional visibility through Instagram and YouTube. The game demonstrates that a large team can deliver a cohesive, themed arcade experience when aligned through strong UX foundations and iterative playtesting.

The project provided valuable experience in scaling UX processes for larger teams, conducting research that informs a shared vision, and balancing thematic goals with genre expectations.

  • Successfully shipped and published on itch.io
  • Cohesive green theme integrated into core gameplay mechanics
  • Coordinated UX vision across a 14-person team
  • Gained experience scaling UX research and design for larger teams
