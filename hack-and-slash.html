Overview

Blending narrative with fast-paced action gameplay

Hack and Slash is an action game set in an office environment where the player uncovers a price-hacking conspiracy. The project required balancing intense combat mechanics with a compelling narrative thread that motivated players to progress.

The UX challenge was twofold: create combat interactions that felt responsive and satisfying, while weaving narrative elements into gameplay without interrupting the flow of action.

Problem

How do you tell a story during non-stop action?

Action games often struggle with the tension between narrative and gameplay. Cutscenes break the flow, but without narrative context, combat becomes repetitive. The challenge was finding ways to deliver story beats within the action itself.

Additionally, the office setting was unusual for an action game. Players needed environmental cues to understand the world and their role in it, without relying on text-heavy exposition.

  • Balancing narrative delivery with uninterrupted action gameplay
  • Making an office setting feel like a compelling game environment
  • Communicating player objectives through environmental storytelling
  • Ensuring combat mechanics supported the narrative theme

Process

Research-driven design with narrative integration

The process began with user research into how action games handle narrative. Competitor analysis of games like Hotline Miami and Katana Zero revealed effective patterns for environmental storytelling in fast-paced contexts.

Narrative elements were mapped onto gameplay beats — story reveals tied to boss encounters, environmental clues scattered through level design, and UI elements that reinforced the corporate thriller theme.

Narrative Design

Developed a story structure that delivered key plot points through gameplay events rather than cutscenes — boss introductions, environmental clues, and progressive reveals.

User Research

Conducted playtesting sessions focused on narrative comprehension — tracking whether players understood the story, their motivations, and the world through gameplay alone.

Narrative Flow Diagram / Playtest Session Documentation

Solution

Environmental storytelling meets responsive combat

The final design used the office environment itself as a narrative device. Desks had documents hinting at the conspiracy, computer screens showed incriminating data, and level progression moved through increasingly suspicious areas of the building.

Combat interactions were designed to feel thematic — using office supplies and equipment as weapons reinforced the setting while maintaining satisfying hack-and-slash mechanics. The UI adopted a corporate aesthetic with clean lines and data-inspired elements.

Final Game UI / Environment Screenshots

Outcome

Narrative clarity through gameplay integration

Post-playtest analysis showed that players who engaged with environmental storytelling elements had significantly better narrative comprehension without any reduction in action engagement. The office setting, initially seen as a constraint, became one of the game's most distinctive features.

The project demonstrated that narrative and action don't have to compete for the player's attention — when integrated thoughtfully, they reinforce each other.

  • Players successfully followed the narrative through environmental cues alone
  • The office setting became a unique and memorable game element
  • Combat mechanics and narrative delivery complemented each other
  • Learned the value of research-driven narrative design in action games
